Problem Description¶

Objective:

Explore dataset to identify differences between customers of each product. Accomplish the following:

  1. Come up with a customer profile (characteristic of a customer) of the different products
  2. Perform univariate and multivariate analysis
  3. Generate a set of insights and recommendations that will help the company in targeting new customers

Data Dictionary:

  1. Product - The model no. of the treadmill
  2. Age - Age of the customer in no of years
  3. Gender - Gender of the customer
  4. Education - Education of the customer in no. of years
  5. Marital Status - Marital status of the customer
  6. Usage - Avg. # times the customer wants to use the treadmill every week
  7. Fitness - Self rated fitness score of the customer (5 - very fit, 1 - very unfit)
  8. Income - Income of the customer
  9. Miles- Miles that a customer expects to run

Conclusion¶

Based off the data, I have made the following observations and recommendations of each model:

TM195:

Characteristics of customers tend to:

  1. Be in the age range of 23-34 years
  2. Have 14-16 years of education
  3. Expect to use treadmill 3-4 times a week and run less than 100 miles a year
  4. Rate themselves as average fitness (3/5)
  5. Have income between roughly $40k - 55k
  6. Equally be male or female

TM498:

Characteristics of customers tend to:

  1. Be in the age range of 24-34 years
  2. Have 14-16 years of education
  3. Expect to use treadmill 3 times a week and have a larger range of miles expected to be ran than the TM195 (roughly between 60-110 miles)
  4. Rate themselves as average fitness (3/5)
  5. Have income between roughly $45k - 55k

TM798:

Characteristics of customers tend to:

  1. Be in the age range of 24-30
  2. Have higher years of education (16-18 years) than the other two models
  3. Have higher expected usage per week and expected miles ran than the other two models
  4. Consider themselves more fit than the other two
  5. Have a higher income and significantly larger income range
  6. Are mainly male customers

Recommendations:

  1. Should focus on targetting the younger demographic (mid 20s to mid 30s)
  2. Based off data, the models should be tiered. The TM798 is the higher tier as it attracts the more serious (higher usage and miles expected to be ran), fit and wealthier demographic. The other two are mid class and have overlapping demographics but the TM195 attracts customers who intend to use the treadmill slightly more per week than the TM498
  3. Customer base is also partnered more than singled
  4. More data should be collected on price per model and it could be useful to understand per state breakdown if this is a national brand/store. Some states like Colorado tend to be more active than others and can help identify opportunistic areas
  5. More data could be gathered on Marital Status. Right now it's limited to partnered and single but could include divorcees
